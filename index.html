About Me

Hi, I'm Niklas. I'm a PhD candidate in the MPI Software Security Group at the Max Planck Institute for Security and Privacy in Bochum, Germany. My research sits at the intersection of software analysis, software synthesis, and machine learning. I'm particularly interested in how learning-based methods can support core program analysis tasks like vulnerability detection — and in designing experiments that reveal what these methods are actually doing versus what they appear to do. My long-term vision is a technique that can synthesize secure software automatically.

Outside of research, I'm co-founder and tech lead of everlense, a web app for sharing wedding photos that today serves around 25k monthly active users with over 2 million image and video uploads each year. Building everlense keeps me close to the engineering reality on the other side of the research — performance, reliability, real users — which I find genuinely useful.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a Software Engineer at the German startup clockin and earned my M.Sc. in Intelligent Systems from Bielefeld University.
